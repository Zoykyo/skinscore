## The six things the whole project is really saying

1. **Prevention beats correction, and it's cheap.** The very top of the leaderboard is dominated by
   *protecting what you already have* — sun protection (95, 94), not smoking (86), not picking (83),
   barrier care (82), sleep (80). These are mostly free or low-cost behaviors, not products. If you
   do nothing else, do these.
2. **A small set of correctors genuinely reverse damage.** Only a handful of interventions have RCT
   evidence for changing the aging phenotype itself: **topical retinoids** (tretinoin 84), and
   in-clinic **botulinum toxin (79), fillers (75), fractional lasers (72)**. Everything else mostly
   maintains or modestly improves.
3. **The "frontier" is sold years ahead of its evidence.** The genuinely futuristic biology —
   **exosomes (43), stem cells (43), senolytics (50), nutrigenomic tests (40)** — clusters at the
   *bottom*. This is the project's central irony: the interventions that best target the root aging
   levers from Cycle 1 (senescence, hormonal decline) have the thinnest human data, while the proven
   treatments only manage symptoms. Watch this space; don't spend on it yet.
4. **Diet and lifestyle punch above their weight.** Because they score high on safety and cost,
   low-glycemic eating (81), polyphenol-rich foods (79), stress reduction (78), and pollution
   hygiene (76) reach Core despite modest effect sizes. Skin is downstream of metabolic and
   inflammatory health.
5. **Baths, heat and cold are mostly the wrong tool for aging.** Their real evidence is
   *condition-specific* (eczema/psoriasis) — soak-and-seal technique (84) and bleach/oatmeal baths —
   or general wellbeing. Notably, **heat is biologically counterproductive for collagen** (it
   upregulates the MMP-1 that degrades it), and **whole-body cryotherapy is the project's only Skip
   (27)**. Enjoy the sauna for your heart, not your face.
6. **Most supplements are Promising-at-best; one is a myth.** Oral **nicotinamide (89)** is the lone
   standout — but only for skin-cancer prevention in high-risk people, not anti-aging. Collagen,
   carotenoids, PLE, and omega-3s are reasonable adjuncts; **biotin (45) does nothing for normal
   skin** and can corrupt lab tests.

## How this becomes a routine

The actionable protocol built from the Core tier (plus a few high-value Promising trials) lives in
**`03_Protocol.md`** — organized into daily AM/PM, weekly, diet, lifestyle, supplements, and a
"procedures to discuss with a dermatologist" section, with the frontier kept on a watchlist.

## Caveats for this first pass

- **Not personalized yet.** Scores are generic. Your skin type, concerns, conditions, medications,
  and budget will move several items (e.g., acne actives, bath interventions for eczema, retinoid
  tolerance). Provide your profile and every row gains a 🟢/🟡/🔴 fit flag.
- **First-pass depth.** Each cycle is a solid first sweep, not the final word. The weekly schedule is
  now repurposed to *deepen* the shallowest cycle each week (more trials, effect sizes, edge cases).
- **Context-dependent scores.** Some interventions are condition-specific (bath therapies for
  eczema/psoriasis score on that basis, not general anti-aging) — noted in their cycle files.
- **Not medical advice.** Prescriptions, procedures, and supplement stacks should be confirmed with a
  dermatologist or physician.
