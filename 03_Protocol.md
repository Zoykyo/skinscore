# The Skin Protocol

*Built from the SkinScore leaderboard (Cycle 9). Everything here is **Core-tier** unless marked
"(trial)" — those are Promising items worth testing one at a time. Frontier items sit on a watchlist,
not in the routine.*

**Status:** first pass, **not yet personalized.** Scores are generic — your skin type, concerns,
conditions, and medications will move several items. Numbers in brackets are SkinScores.
**Not medical advice:** confirm prescriptions, procedures, and supplement stacks with a dermatologist.

---

## Daily — morning ☀️
- [ ] **Gentle cleanse** (or just rinse with water if skin is dry) — avoid over-stripping [81]
- [ ] **Vitamin C serum** (L-ascorbic acid) — antioxidant, supports the SPF [75, trial]
- [ ] **Moisturizer with ceramides** — barrier support [82]
- [ ] **Broad-spectrum sunscreen SPF 30+** — the single highest-value step; reapply if outdoors [95]

## Daily — evening 🌙
- [ ] **Cleanse** to remove sunscreen + the day's pollution particulates [76]
- [ ] **A retinoid** — the one proven topical that reverses photoaging. Prescription **tretinoin** [84]
      is strongest; **OTC retinol** [75] or **adapalene 0.1%** [77] are solid starts. Begin 2×/week,
      build up. ⚠️ Avoid in pregnancy; expect an adjustment period.
- [ ] **Niacinamide** — barrier + tone + oil; pairs well with almost everything [82]
- [ ] **Moisturizer / ceramide barrier** [82]
- [ ] *Acne-prone:* **benzoyl peroxide** [83] and/or **azelaic acid** [75] or **salicylic acid** [75]
      as directed (don't stack all actives at once)

## Weekly / periodic
- [ ] **Don't over-exfoliate** — at most 1–2× gentle AHA/BHA if tolerated [68, trial]; over-exfoliation
      damages the barrier
- [ ] **Red / near-infrared LED** device, a few sessions/week — modest but real for collagen [74, trial]
- [ ] If you take baths: **soak-and-seal** — short, lukewarm, moisturize immediately after [84]

## Diet — ongoing 🥗
- [ ] **Low glycemic load** — fewer sugar spikes; targets glycation + acne [81]
- [ ] **Polyphenol-rich foods** — green tea, cocoa flavanols, tomatoes/lycopene, colorful produce [79]
- [ ] **Mediterranean pattern** overall; **fermented foods + fiber** for the gut–skin axis [73]
- [ ] **Oily fish / omega-3s** a few times a week [71]
- [ ] **Limit** high-AGE cooking (deep-fried, char-grilled), added sugar, and alcohol [72]
- [ ] *Acne-prone:* trial **reducing dairy**, especially skim milk [75]

## Lifestyle — ongoing 🧬
- [ ] **Sun-protective behavior** — shade, hats, UV-blocking clothing, avoid midday peak [94]
- [ ] **Don't smoke** [86]
- [ ] **Sleep 7–9 h**, consistently [80]
- [ ] **Manage stress** (cortisol drives collagen loss + barrier damage) [78]
- [ ] **Don't pick or over-touch** your face [83]
- [ ] Minor helpers: **regular exercise** [70], **indoor humidity ~40–50%** [67, trial]

## Supplements — evidence-gated (optional) 💊
- [ ] **Vitamin D** — if you're deficient (test first) [74]
- [ ] **Oral nicotinamide** 500 mg 2×/day — ⚠️ **only if you're at high skin-cancer risk**, and discuss
      with a dermatologist; not a general anti-aging pill [89, narrow indication]
- [ ] *Reasonable trials, modest effects:* collagen peptides [71], mixed carotenoids / Polypodium
      leucotomos for photoprotection adjunct [71], omega-3 [69], zinc if acne-prone [71]
- [ ] **Skip: biotin** — does nothing for normal skin and can corrupt lab tests [45]

## Procedures — discuss with a dermatologist 🏥
*Higher cost/effort, but real, RCT-backed correction for those who want it:*
- Botulinum toxin [79] · hyaluronic acid fillers [75] · fractional laser [72] · microneedling ± RF [70]
  · IPL for redness/pigment [68]

## Frontier watchlist — NOT recommended yet 🔬
*Biologically exciting, evidence too thin to spend on. Revisit as human data matures:*
- Senolytics (dasatinib+quercetin) [50] · topical rapamycin [60] · exosomes [43] · stem-cell cosmetics
  [43] · topical growth factors [54] · nutrigenomic/at-home genetic tests [40]

## Explicitly not worth it ❌
- **Whole-body cryotherapy for skin** [27] — the only Skip; unproven and carries real risk.
- Saunas/steam/ice facials for *aging* [45–48] — fine for wellbeing, not for your face's collagen.

---

### How to use this
Start with the **Core** daily/lifestyle/diet items — that's ~90% of the achievable benefit at low
cost. Add **(trial)** items one at a time, a few weeks each, so you can tell what actually helps
*your* skin. When you share your skin profile, each line gets a 🟢 good-fit / 🟡 careful / 🔴 avoid
flag, and the acne-specific and bath items get tuned to your situation.
